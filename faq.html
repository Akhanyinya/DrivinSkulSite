Yes, you can start learning to drive if you are under 18 years old. However, you will need parental consent and may have certain restrictions on your driving privileges until you turn 18.

To obtain a driving license in Kenya, you must be at least 18 years old and have completed a driving course at a registered driving school. You will also need to pass both the theory and practical driving tests conducted by the National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA).

The validity of a Kenyan driving license depends on the type of license issued. Generally, private vehicle licenses are valid for 3 years, while commercial vehicle licenses need to be renewed annually. It is essential to renew your license before it expires to avoid penalties.

To enroll in a driving school in Kenya, you will need to bring your national ID, a copy of your KRA PIN certificate, a passport-sized photo, and proof of payment for the driving course.

The cost of the driving course may vary depending on the type of license you are applying for and the driving school you choose. Please contact our office or visit us to get detailed information about our course fees and packages.
